  • Camera Setup Basics

    Start with Aperture Priority mode. Use ISO 100–200 in daylight and adjust shutter speed depending on movement. Always check focus before shooting.

  • Understanding Exposure

    Balance aperture, shutter speed, and ISO. A wider aperture (f/1.8) creates background blur, while higher shutter speed freezes motion.

  • Natural Light Tips

    Shoot during golden hour for soft light. Avoid harsh midday sun. Cloudy days create smooth and even lighting for portraits.

  • Using Artificial Light

    Use softboxes or ring lights for soft shadows. Avoid direct flash. Diffusers help create more natural-looking images.

  • Shooting in Low Light

    Increase ISO carefully, open aperture wider (f/1.8–f/2.8), and use a tripod to prevent blur from slow shutter speeds.

  • Outdoor Photography

    Position your subject facing the light source. Use shadows creatively and avoid strong backlighting unless for silhouette effects.

  • Composition Rules

    Use the rule of thirds, leading lines, and keep the background simple. Focus attention on one main subject.

  • Focus Settings

    Use Single AF for still subjects and Continuous AF for moving objects. Always double-check sharpness before taking the final shot.

  • Beginner Practice Tip

    Practice in different lighting conditions. Review your photos, analyze mistakes, and adjust camera settings step by step.
